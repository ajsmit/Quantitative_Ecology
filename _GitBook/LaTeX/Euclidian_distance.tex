\section{Definition}

The Euclidean distance between points \textbf{p} and \textbf{q} is the
length of the line segment connecting them
(\(\overline{\mathbf{p}\mathbf{q}}\)).

On an Euclidian plane, the position of point \textbf{p} is defined by
the position \((p_{x},p_{y})\) and the position of point q is
\((q_{x},q_{y})\). The shortest distance between the points is therefore
given by the Pythagorean Theorem

\[ d(\mathbf{p},\mathbf{q}) = \sqrt{(p_{x} - q_{x})^{2} + (p_{y} - q_{y})^2} \]
